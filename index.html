Research Experience

1. Ultrasonic-Driven Wetting Control in Dip Soldering for Micro LED Packaging

Research Assistant / Co-First Author | IMLab | 2025

  • Context: Addressed the limitation of conventional Fluidic Self-Assembly (FSA) methods in forming fine solder bumps (<10 µm) for Micro LED displays.
  • Methodology: Developed Ultrasonic-Assisted Dip Soldering (UADS) technology to enhance solder wetting on Au pads.
  • Key Achievements:
    • Successfully eliminated internal gas voids and oxide layers using ultrasonic cavitation energy.
    • Achieved stable formation of sub-10 µm micro-solder bumps, significantly improving alignment precision and bonding yield for AR/VR display applications.

2. Integration of PDMS Microfluidic Cooling for CPO-PIC Platforms

Team Lead / Capstone Design Project | Hanyang Univ. | 2025

  • Context: Solved thermal bottlenecks in 3D-stacked semiconductors (HBM & Co-Packaged Optics) where heat degrades refractive index stability.
  • Methodology: Designed a Microfluidic Cooling Channel using PDMS between the Electronic IC (EIC) and Photonic IC (PIC).
  • Key Achievements:
    • Conducted thermal and optical simulations to optimize the thickness of the PDMS layer (>3 µm) to minimize optical loss in waveguides.
    • Demonstrated an efficient heat dissipation structure that maintains signal integrity in high-performance computing environments.

3. Wafer Defect Pattern Recognition & Yield Analysis

Data Analyst / Independent Project | 2024

  • Methodology: Utilized Python to process wafer map coordinate data.
  • Key Achievements:
    • Data Preprocessing: Cleaned and structured raw fab data using Pandas.
    • Visualization: Developed automated scripts using Matplotlib to generate wafer maps, identifying specific defect signatures (Scratch, Ring, Edge, Random).
    • Insight: Calculated process yield (79.25%) and correlated defect clusters with potential equipment anomalies.

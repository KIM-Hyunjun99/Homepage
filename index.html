About Me

I am a graduate of Hanyang University ERICA with a degree in Nano-optoelectronics, specializing in next-generation semiconductor packaging. My primary interest lies in developing innovative packaging technologies for Micro LED displays and silicon photonics.

I am particularly focused on developing new bonding technologies using ultrasonic energy and optimizing process yields through data analysis with Python. From thermal management in 3D-IC structures to solving fine-pitch interconnect issues for Micro LEDs, I aim to contribute to cutting-edge semiconductor solutions based on technical rigor.

Research

PDMS-based Micro-Fluidic Cooling Channel

To address the critical heat dissipation issues in 3D-stacked semiconductors (HBM, CPO), I designed a microfluidic cooling channel using PDMS. Through thermal and optical simulations, I optimized the structure to efficiently dissipate heat while minimizing optical loss in waveguides, thereby maintaining signal integrity in high-performance computing environments.

Ultrasonic-Assisted Dip Soldering (UADS)

To overcome the limitations of conventional Fluidic Self-Assembly (FSA) and to reliably form solder bumps smaller than 10Âµm, I developed the Ultrasonic-Assisted Dip Soldering (UADS) technology. By using ultrasonic cavitation energy to effectively remove oxide layers and internal voids from the solder, this technique significantly improves alignment precision and bonding yield for AR/VR display packaging.

Micro LED Chip Fabrication & Process Analysis

With a strong foundation in the 8 major semiconductor processes, I fabricate Micro LED chips and analyze process outcomes. This involves understanding the mechanism of each piece of equipment and optimizing process recipes. I also perform automated defect recognition on fabricated wafers, using Python (Pandas, Matplotlib) to visualize wafer map data and correlate defect clusters with potential equipment anomalies to enhance process yield.
